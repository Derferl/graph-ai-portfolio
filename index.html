About Me

I am a global health professional with over 12 years of experience managing multi-sectoral public health programs across Africa. My expertise spans complex project management, institutional grant management, and data-driven insights that inform adaptive program strategies. I have co-authored national health policies, implemented digital data systems, and supported capacity strengthening across multiple countries.

Currently serving as Monitoring, Evaluation, and Learning (MEL) Advisor at Project HOPE’s Global Team, I lead regional assessments, data system innovations, and cross-country learning initiatives. My focus is on leveraging AI tools to enhance efficiency, evidence-based decision-making, and research excellence in global health.

About the Course

Generative AI for Work & Research is an 8-week live cohort course by GRAPH Courses at the University of Geneva, Switzerland. It introduces professionals to no-code AI tools that improve productivity, automate workflows, and transform research and communication.

  • Weekly live workshops & help sessions
  • Capstone project guidance
  • Lifetime access to tutorials
  • Global peer collaboration

Modules include: AI for writing and presentation design, automated data analysis, literature review and text synthesis, custom GPT creation, workflow automation, and AI for research communication.
